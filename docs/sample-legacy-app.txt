ACME Inventory Management System (IMS) — Legacy Application Overview
=====================================================================

1. SYSTEM OVERVIEW

The ACME Inventory Management System has been in production since 2009. It tracks
warehouse stock levels, purchase orders, supplier relationships, and shipping
fulfillment for ACME Corp's three distribution centers located in Dallas, Chicago,
and Newark. The system processes approximately 12,000 transactions per day and is
used by 85 warehouse staff, 20 procurement analysts, and 10 finance team members.

2. TECHNOLOGY STACK

- Backend: Java 8 (Oracle JDK) running on Apache Tomcat 7.0.
- Database: Oracle 11g R2 with approximately 140 tables and 2,200 stored procedures.
  The database is 1.4 TB and growing at roughly 15 GB per month.
- Frontend: JSP pages with jQuery 1.8 and custom JavaScript. No responsive design.
- Reporting: Crystal Reports 2013, generating 45 scheduled reports nightly.
- Integration: SOAP web services for ERP (SAP) and accounting (QuickBooks Enterprise).
  FTP-based file transfers for EDI 850/856 documents with major suppliers.
- Infrastructure: Two physical Dell PowerEdge R720 servers (primary + failover) in
  the Dallas data center. No containerization or cloud presence.
- Authentication: LDAP integration with Microsoft Active Directory. Single sign-on
  is not implemented; users maintain separate IMS credentials.

3. CORE FEATURES

- Real-time stock tracking with barcode scanning (Honeywell scanners via serial port).
- Purchase order creation, approval workflows (three-tier: analyst → manager → VP for
  orders exceeding $50,000), and supplier communication via automated email.
- Receiving dock management: inbound shipment logging, quality inspection checklists,
  and automated stock level updates upon receipt confirmation.
- Pick-pack-ship workflow with wave planning for outbound orders. Supports FIFO and
  FEFO (First Expired, First Out) picking strategies for perishable goods.
- Cycle counting module that schedules daily counts by zone rotation.
- Supplier scorecard tracking delivery accuracy, lead time, and defect rates.

4. BUSINESS RULES

- Minimum stock thresholds trigger automatic reorder suggestions. The reorder point
  formula is: (average daily usage × lead time days) + safety stock percentage.
  Safety stock is set per SKU category: 20% for standard items, 35% for critical items.
- Items classified as hazardous materials (HAZMAT) require additional documentation
  fields and cannot be stored in zones A or B due to proximity to office areas.
- Purchase orders above $50,000 require VP approval. Orders above $100,000 require
  both VP and CFO approval. There is no documented policy for orders above $500,000.
- Returns from customers must be processed within 48 hours of receipt. Returned items
  go through a three-step inspection: visual check, functional test, repackaging
  assessment. Items failing functional test are routed to the write-off queue.
- Lot tracking is mandatory for pharmaceutical and food-grade product categories.
  Each lot must maintain full chain-of-custody records from receipt to shipment.

5. KNOWN ISSUES AND PAIN POINTS

- The Oracle stored procedures contain significant business logic that is poorly
  documented. Several procedures reference a "LEGACY_CALC" package whose original
  developer left the company in 2015. No one fully understands the margin calculation
  logic inside LEGACY_CALC.PR_CALCULATE_MARGIN.
- Performance degrades significantly during month-end closing. Inventory valuation
  reports that should take 10 minutes now take over 2 hours.
- The jQuery frontend is not mobile-friendly. Warehouse staff using tablets must
  zoom and scroll constantly, leading to scanning errors.
- FTP-based EDI integration fails silently. Failed transfers are only discovered
  when suppliers call to complain about missing purchase orders.
- There is no audit trail for manual stock adjustments. Several discrepancies
  between physical counts and system records remain unexplained.
- The failover server has not been tested since 2021. DR procedures are undocumented.
